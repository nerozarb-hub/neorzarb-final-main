## Design Context

### Users
NEROZARB serves founders, operators, and growth-minded business owners who are tired of vague agency retainers and need a proof-first digital growth system. Primary contexts are Pakistan, Gulf, and Azerbaijan markets, with many leads entering through fast WhatsApp conversations after seeing the website, social proof, or a direct pitch. Users need to understand what NEROZARB does, trust that the team can execute, see credible proof, and book a next-step call or WhatsApp conversation without friction.

### Brand Personality
The brand voice is the Accessible Architect: clear, precise, proof-led, and direct without becoming cold. The interface should feel like a sharp operating room for growth systems: engineered, anti-agency, confident, and commercially practical. Personality words: surgical, grounded, uncompromising.

### Aesthetic Direction
The current system points toward a dark, hard-edged command-center aesthetic: sharp 90-degree geometry, forest green accents, restrained motion, and no soft SaaS gloss. Keep the site dark because prospects encounter it during research, pitch follow-up, and decision-making contexts where a high-contrast proof room fits the brand. Avoid generic AI-site tells: gradient text, glowing cyan/purple dark mode, rounded glass cards, repeated icon-card grids, and vague hero metrics.

### Design Principles
- Lead with conversion clarity: every section should move a skeptical buyer toward proof, offer understanding, qualification, and booking.
- Show proof before adjectives: case studies, outcomes, systems, client context, and screenshots should carry credibility more than broad claims.
- Keep the visual language sharp: square geometry, controlled green accents, strong hierarchy, and no unnecessary softness.
- Make booking effortless: sticky primary action, clear WhatsApp route, and a lower-friction consult path should be visible at key decision points.
- Use motion as guidance: purposeful entrance, hover, and scroll feedback only; preserve performance and respect reduced-motion preferences.
